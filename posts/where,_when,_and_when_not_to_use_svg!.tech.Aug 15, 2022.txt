<p id="1ce9" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">In a&nbsp;<a class="ay ot" href="https://medium.com/codex/creating-custom-icon-webfonts-478a934c7ddf" rel="noopener">previous article I wrote about using webfonts for icons.</a>&nbsp;This is a powerful technique great for monochrome icons without pissing all over the HTML. But it also caused a few people to ask me &ldquo;So you wouldn&rsquo;t use SVG ever?&rdquo;</p>
<p id="072c" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">Of course I&rsquo;d use SVG. It&rsquo;s more a matter of when, where, and why. Though if you should use it is also a good question and very situation specific. I think in web development we have far too many people adopting the attitude of &ldquo;well so and so said to use this, so it&rsquo;s the answer to everything.&rdquo;</p>
<p id="dc8c" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">That is rarely the case outside of the basic rules of the markup and styling languages themselves.</p>
<h1 id="ed9c" class="acu acv zn bv ky acw acx acy tl acz aea aeb tq tr aec ts tv tw aed tx ua ub aee uc uf aef by" data-selectable-paragraph="">When Shouldn&rsquo;t We Use SVG?</h1>
<p id="5601" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aeg acb acc acd aeh acf acg ach aei acj ack acl aej acn aco acp aek acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">The number one case of where to avoid is presentational monochrome images such as flat icons. Webfonts can be cached, the two modern formats of WOFF and WOFF2 compress much smaller than gzipping minified SVG does by the very nature of their storing things like numbers in binary instead of as text, etc, etm.</p>
<p id="531d" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">Using more than one color in the image though throws a monkey-wrench in the works. Whilst multi-color fonts are coming along, support isn&rsquo;t robust enough to deploy reliably, generating them is a bit tricky compared to SVG and oft requires paying more for &ldquo;pro&rdquo; versions of font creation software.</p>
<p id="ebe9" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">If you want a multi-color vector images webfonts are a bad choice compared to SVG.&nbsp;<em class="ael">Though there are ways around said issue. I should write an an article about that.</em></p>
<p id="2906" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">Really that&rsquo;s the only argument against using SVG that comes to mind. You get smaller files and better caching when the images are presentational. Bringing us to one simple truth:</p>
<h1 id="7328" class="acu acv zn bv ky acw acx acy tl acz aea aeb tq tr aec ts tv tw aed tx ua ub aee uc uf aef by" data-selectable-paragraph="">Is It Presentational And/Or Decoration? Then Don&rsquo;t Put It In The Markup!</h1>
<p id="982d" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aeg acb acc acd aeh acf acg ach aei acj ack acl aej acn aco acp aek acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">This is the number one place I see people screw things up. Images like logo&rsquo;s and icons are&nbsp;<strong class="ug he">not</strong>&nbsp;content, they are a presentational affectation of or around the content. They also tend to be used across multiple page loads. To that end if you put them in your stylesheet or external files you increase the odds of them being cached, not only speeding up the loading of subpages, but also reducing your server load!</p>
<p id="e13d" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph=""><em class="ael">I&rsquo;ve said it time and time again, most of the &ldquo;pageloads r teh evuls&rdquo; BS making people dive for inaccessible SPA rubbish for things that shouldn&rsquo;t need scripted nonsense in the first place is born of failing to use HTML properly.</em></p>
<p id="49cf" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">This is a place a lot of people who crap endless garbage like SVG icons or presentational classes &mdash;&nbsp;<em class="ael">yes,&nbsp;</em><strong class="ug he"><em class="ael">presentational,&nbsp;</em></strong><em class="ael">not that &ldquo;utility&rdquo; propaganda bullshit&nbsp;</em>&mdash; into their HTML end up either ignorant of, or making lame excuses to justify. It&rsquo;s not just about client side time. The more markup you make your back end have to paste together the longer you are holding the connection open. This &ldquo;oh it&rsquo;ll be gzipped&rdquo; or &ldquo;it&rsquo;s only a few dozen k&rdquo; poppycock adds up when you have large numbers of connections going.</p>
<p id="3dfa" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">The more you can take out of the markup and into an external file, the faster you can serve the markup, the faster it can render, and the massive amount of bandwidth you can save on high traffic multi-page sites through caching. Anyone telling you other wise is bending you over a log and telling you to &ldquo;squeal like a pig boy&rdquo; like poor Ned Beatty on a river adventure.</p>
<p id="ea7d" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph=""><strong class="ug he">Now that doesn&rsquo;t mean don&rsquo;t ever use SVG in the markup</strong>, but seriously load it via IMG or PICTURE from an external file if you can. Slopping static SVG&rsquo;s markup into your HTML is just dumbass halfwitted ignorant trash.&nbsp;<em class="ael">Even with the &ldquo;it saves a few handshakes&rdquo; concepts in the mix.</em></p>
<p id="e4f7" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">But like any good rule, there is an exception. Putting it in the markup allows it to be manipulated by JavaScript and CSS. If the image is content, actual data, not decoration, and you plan on needing to manipulate its values from JS or do extensive external stylesheet styling,&nbsp;<strong class="ug he">then you go right ahead</strong>&nbsp;and put it in your HTML.</p>
<p id="9227" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph=""><em class="ael">Though if it&rsquo;s scripting only, I would consider generating / adding it from inside the scripting so non-JS capable UA&rsquo;s aren&rsquo;t confusing the user with crap that doesn&rsquo;t apply to them.</em></p>
<h1 id="6485" class="acu acv zn bv ky acw acx acy tl acz aea aeb tq tr aec ts tv tw aed tx ua ub aee uc uf aef by" data-selectable-paragraph="">If Not In The Markup, Where?</h1>

Since any well written site&nbsp;<em class="ael">should</em><strong class="ug he"><em class="ael">&nbsp;</em></strong>have most if not all its CSS in a single stylesheet per media target, it&rsquo;s not like that handshake is going anywhere.</p>
<h2 id="cd52" class="aer acv zn bv ky th aew ti tl tm aex tn tq ach aey aez tv acl afa afb ua acp afc afd uf afe by" data-selectable-paragraph="">What Do You Mean &ldquo;Media Target&rdquo;</h2>
<p id="4d63" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aeg acb acc acd aeh acf acg ach aei acj ack acl aej acn aco acp aek acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">One of the entire reasons CSS exists is to be able to declare style for different device types. It&rsquo;s not all &ldquo;screen appearance screw everybody else&rdquo; no matter how many ignorant turds design sites that way. The &lt;link&gt; and &lt;style&gt; tags have a media attribute you should be using to say&nbsp;<strong class="ug he">what the stylesheet is for!</strong></p>
<p id="2994" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">For example:</p>
<pre class="aem aen aeo aep yg ol jw aeq"><span id="cabf" class="by aer acv zn aes b ec aet aeu y aev" data-selectable-paragraph="">&lt;link rel="stylesheet" media="screen" href="demo.screen.css"&gt;<br>&lt;link rel="stylesheet" media="print" href="demo.print.css"&gt;</span></pre>

<h2 id="0f8d" class="aer acv zn bv ky th aew ti tl tm aex tn tq ach aey aez tv acl afa afb ua acp afc afd uf afe by" data-selectable-paragraph="">The Problem With SVG Backgrounds</h2>
<p id="ccae" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aeg acb acc acd aeh acf acg ach aei acj ack acl aej acn aco acp aek acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">You have zero control over the colouration using CSS variables or properties. If you&nbsp;<strong class="ug he">really</strong>&nbsp;need that functionality, you might make an exception to the rule. Just be bloody damned sure you&rsquo;re not pissing on why HTML exists and what it&rsquo;s for by violating the separation of presentation (CSS) from content (HTML)!</p>

<h1 id="0364" class="acu acv zn bv ky acw acx acy tl acz aea aeb tq tr aec ts tv tw aed tx ua ub aee uc uf aef by" data-selectable-paragraph="">Conclusion</h1>
<p id="0540" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aeg acb acc acd aeh acf acg ach aei acj ack acl aej acn aco acp aek acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">There are many pros and cons to SVG. They are slower rendering than fonts, the file sizes are usually bigger, and if used in the markup you&rsquo;re causing all sorts of bandwidth and speed issues. At the same time their ability to be scaled, have multiple colours and even gradient shading, embed images as textures on their vectors, manipulating them from JavaScript, and host of other capabilities make them hard to ignore or avoid entirely. Nor should you.</p>
<p id="ffef" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph=""><strong class="ug he">It&rsquo;s not about &ldquo;don&rsquo;t ever use them&rdquo; or &ldquo;always use them!&rdquo;</strong>&nbsp;It&rsquo;s about learning how to use them properly, when to use them in the first place, and when alternatives are better or worse.</p>
<p id="fd38" class="pw-post-body-paragraph abx aby zn ug b abz aca acb acc acd ace acf acg ach aci acj ack acl acm acn aco acp acq acr acs act qq by" data-selectable-paragraph="">I wish more people understood that about web technologies, instead of this crazy &ldquo;learn one way right or wrong and apply it to everything&rdquo; attitude that&rsquo;s all to painfully common.&nbsp;<a class="ay ot" href="https://deathshadow.medium.com/stop-turning-avoid-because-or-when-appropriate-into-never-use-the-alternative-fa42e72d02d2" rel="noopener">As I&rsquo;ve said for years&nbsp;</a>there&rsquo;s this horrible habit people have of turning &ldquo;don&rsquo;t use X when Y is more appropriate&rdquo; into &ldquo;don&rsquo;t ever use X&rdquo;.</p>
